External Links

Programming

Suckless.org
This site offers many minimal alternatives to various programs. Of this group of programmers, I especially like st, the simple terminal emulator, and dwm, a very simple tiled window manager for the X Window System.
Chuck Moore's Webpage
The webpage of FORTH's inventor, where I found many interesting things. What I like most about this page is that it contains true pieces of the life of a man who really loved his work and his family. A very concise version of the FORTH programming system named ColorForth is also introduced.
Forth Interest Group
This site has been the starting point for finding information about the Forth programming language. There are many useful references and literature from which you can learn about it.
CAT -V Harmful Things
Here, interesting topics in Computer Science are debated and discussed with a touch of sarcasm. The important message of this site is to remind us not to idolize high-complexity solutions and to prefer straightforward simplicity.
Floodgap
A site about very old stuff, discovered while looking for information about the Gopher protocol. If you're interested, you can connect to their gopherhole directly or through their HTTP proxy.

Webmail Providers

Tuta (Tutanota)
A privacy-focused webmail provider. They offer a free plan for your personal email address, with no ads, full encryption, and no data sharing with third-party companies.
